Plank Detection

Published Jul 8, 2020 by at https://i.cs.hku.hk/~msp19047/plank/

When it comes to the view on iPhone’s screen, the coordinate system of ARView rotates a certain angle when the user is doing the plank. For the user, the hip joint still served as an origin and the direction of the head is along the positive Y-axis, as illustrated below.

Coordinate System Rotates in Plank

The essential point of the plank is that the trunk and legs should lie in the same plane. ARCoach judge whether the user is doing plank correctly by tracking 5 joints, head, left shoulder, right shoulder, left foot, and right foot on Z-axis. ARCoach calculates the distance between the user’s joint data and model’s joint data which is extracted from a professional coach. If the absolute difference value from both parties is close to 0 for all 5 joints, it implies these key body joints are nearly at the same Z plane.

To raise a hint, ARCoach defines a threshold at Z-axis and the error of the user’s joint data would be compared to the threshold at each frame. The hint would be “pay attention to xxx joint” where xxx is concluded after comparisons of all 5 joints.

The reason why ARKit is not stable at detecting plank might be that some joints couldn’t be seen through the back camera of the iPhone. The disturbance caused by unstable detecting would affect the judgment and hints of ARCoach upon the plank.
